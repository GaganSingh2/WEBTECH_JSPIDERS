✨Instruction For Made a Channa-Rice
        🥘 Ingredients (for 3–4 servings)
        For Channa (Chole Curry)
        Chickpeas (Channa / Kabuli Chana) – 1 cup (soaked overnight)
        Onion – 2 medium (finely chopped)
        Tomato – 2 medium (pureed)
        Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
        Green chilies – 1–2 (slit)
        Chole masala – 2 tsp (store-bought or homemade)
        Red chili powder – 1 tsp
        Turmeric powder – ¼ tsp
        Coriander powder – 1 tsp
        Garam masala – ½ tsp
        Cumin seeds – ½ tsp
        Bay leaf – 1
        Tea bag / black tea leaves (optional, for dark color)
        Salt – to taste
        Oil / ghee – 3 tbsp
        Fresh coriander – 2 tbsp (for garnish)
        For Rice:
        Basmati rice – 1½ cup
        Cumin seeds – ½ tsp (optional, for jeera rice)
        Salt – to taste
        Water – 3 cups

        👩‍🍳 Preparation Steps
        1. Cook Chickpeas:->
        Soak chickpeas overnight.
        Pressure cook with salt + a pinch of baking soda (and tea bag if using) for 5–6 whistles, until soft.
        Drain and keep aside (remove tea bag).

        2. Prepare Chole Masala:->
        Heat oil in a kadhai/pan.
        Add cumin seeds & bay leaf.
        Add onions, sauté till golden brown.
        Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies → sauté.
        Add tomato puree, cook till oil separates.
        Mix in turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, chole masala.
        Add boiled chickpeas + 1 cup water (use stock from boiled chana).
        Simmer 15–20 minutes until thick & flavorful.
        Sprinkle garam masala & garnish with coriander leaves.

        3. Cook Rice:->
        Wash & soak rice for 20 minutes.
        In a pan, boil 3 cups water with salt (and cumin seeds if making jeera rice).
        Add rice & cook till fluffy (or pressure cook for 2 whistles).
